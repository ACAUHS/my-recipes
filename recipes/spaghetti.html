Spaghetti Recipe

Description

Back when I lived with my parents, my Dad would always make this spaghetti recipe whenever it was a special occasion. Although it's not considered a traditional spaghetti recipe, it's definitely one of my favourite variations on the dish. The main difference in this recipe however, is the addition of banana ketchup in the sauce. I promise it does not taste as disgusting as it sounds, and instead it adds a bit of sweetness/tang to the recipe, that you wouldn't normally get with a traditional recipe. I wouldn't completely classify it as Filipino spaghetti though, considering how that is very sweet and usually has the addition of sausages.

Ingredients

  • Spaghettini
  • Ground beef
  • Spaghetti sauce with mushrooms
  • 1/2 onion
  • 3-4 cloves chopped garlic
  • 3-cheese mix
  • Banana ketchup
  • Beef bouillon
  • Cajun mix

Steps

  1. Put ground beef in pan, on medium heat
  2. Break up ground beef and cook until no more water excretes and oil comes out instead
  3. Set aside in separate bowl (KEEP THE OIL IN THE PAN)
  4. Sautee garlic 10-20 seconds and immediately add onions for around 5 minutes
    • Make sure onions are caramelized (translucent colour)
  5. Put ground beef back in with the sautee and add 1 beef bouillon cube
  6. Sautee for another 3-4 minutes
  7. Put spaghetti sauce in with mixture and add 1-2 tablespoons of banana ketchup
  8. Add cajun seasoning
  9. Simmer
  10. Add cooked spaghettini and mix
  11. Add 3-cheese mix
